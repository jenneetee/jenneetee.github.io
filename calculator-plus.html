CALCULATOR+ MOBILE APP CONCEPT

Project Summary

Program Used: Figma

2nd Place Winner of HackHer, the First Ever All-Female Hackathon at Georgia State hosted by Girls Who Code at GSU, Fall 2023

A mobile app concept following the Fall 2023 HackHers theme, "Women's Safety," where each team was tasked to come up with a project that could help improve women's safety on campus, online, at home, in public, and in the workplace. My team's idea was an emergency response app that disguises itself as a calculator. Once the user inputs a secret PIN, the app will take her to an alternate screen that lists quick-response resources for when someone finds herself in a dangerous situation that requires discreet aid. Some of these functions include pre-recorded phone audio, a map with safe routes, and emergency hotline numbers.

Lessons Learned

I learned the basics of Figma within a few hours. During the process, I was able to add triggering paths to buttons, pop-up menus/overlays, and other features. Additionally, I worked in a team which allowed me to improve my teambuilding skills and learn to work together in a cooperative manner within a short period. Each team member was assigned a specific role in the design process of the wireframe, such as designing the layout, adding button functionality, and demo testing. The lesson I learned from this project was the importance of time management. Since the project was for a hackathon, we had to prioritize demonstrating its main functionalities over aesthetics to communicate its purpose effectively to the judges.
